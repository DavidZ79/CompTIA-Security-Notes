> [!quote] Definition
> A **security control** is a safeguard or countermeasure designed to protect the **confidentiality**, **integrity**, and **availability** (CIA triad) of information systems and data.

## Control Categories

Security controls are typically organized into four main categories based on their implementation method:

<details>
<summary><strong>Technical Controls</strong></summary>

*Implemented through technology and technical systems of the organization*

**Examples:**
- **Firewalls** - Network traffic filtering and monitoring
- **Data Encryption** - Protecting data at rest and in transit
- **Intrusion Detection Systems (IDS)** - Monitoring for suspicious activity
- **Antivirus Software** - Malware detection and prevention
- **Access Control Systems** - Authentication and authorization mechanisms
- **Network Segmentation** - Isolating network segments for security

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Managerial Controls</strong></summary>

*Implemented by management through policies, procedures, and practices to direct individuals and teams*

**Examples:**
- **Performance Reviews** - Regular assessment of security practices
- **Risk Assessments** - Systematic evaluation of security risks
- **Code of Conduct** - Ethical guidelines for employees
- **Security Policies** - Formal documentation of security requirements
- **Vendor Management** - Oversight of third-party security practices
- **Budget Allocation** - Resource planning for security initiatives

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Operational Controls</strong></summary>

*Implemented with day-to-day processes and procedures*

**Examples:**
- **Incident Response Procedures** - Steps to handle security incidents
- **Security Awareness Training** - Regular education for employees
- **User Access Management** - Onboarding/offboarding processes
- **Backup Procedures** - Regular data backup and recovery testing
- **Change Management** - Controlled modification of systems
- **Vulnerability Management** - Regular security assessments

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Physical Controls</strong></summary>

*Implemented using tangible measures to protect organization assets*

**Examples:**
- **Access Control Vestibules** - Controlled entry points
- **Biometric Locks** - Fingerprint, retinal, or facial recognition
- **Security Guards** - Human monitoring and response
- **Security Fences** - Perimeter protection
- **CCTV Surveillance Systems** - Video monitoring and recording
- **Mantraps** - Controlled access through dual-door systems
- **Vehicle Barriers** - Physical obstacles for vehicle access
- **Tamper-Evident Seals** - Visual indicators of unauthorized access
- **Panic Buttons/Alarms** - Emergency notification systems
- **Environmental Controls** - HVAC, fire suppression, power backup

</details>

## Control Types

Security controls can also be classified by their function and timing:

<details>
<summary><strong>Preventative Controls</strong></summary>

*Designed to prevent security incidents before they occur*

**Examples:**
- Firewalls and intrusion prevention systems
- Access controls and authentication
- Data encryption
- Security awareness training
- Physical barriers and locks

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Deterrent Controls</strong></summary>

*Designed to discourage potential attackers*

**Examples:**
- Warning signs and notices
- Security cameras (visible)
- Security guards in uniform
- Posted policies and consequences
- Audit trails and monitoring notices

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Detective Controls</strong></summary>

*Designed to identify and detect security incidents*

**Examples:**
- Intrusion detection systems
- Log monitoring and analysis
- Security event monitoring
- Audit trails and logs
- Video surveillance recording

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Corrective Controls</strong></summary>

*Implemented after an incident to mitigate the impact*

**Examples:**
- Incident response procedures
- Disaster recovery plans
- System restoration processes
- Patch management
- Backup restoration

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Compensating Controls</strong></summary>

*Alternative measures to compensate for inadequate primary controls*

**Examples:**
- Additional monitoring when encryption isn't available
- Manual review processes for automated systems
- Temporary security measures during system upgrades
- Enhanced logging when access controls are limited

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Directive Controls</strong></summary>

*Policies and regulations that guide behavior*

**Examples:**
- Acceptable use policies
- Data classification policies
- Regulatory compliance requirements
- Industry standards and guidelines
- Organizational security policies

</details>



> [!tip] Control Selection Considerations
> - **Risk Level** - Match control strength to threat level
> - **Cost-Benefit** - Balance security with operational efficiency  
> - **Layered Defense** - Implement multiple controls for critical assets
> - **User Impact** - Minimize disruption to legitimate users
> - **Maintenance** - Consider ongoing support and update requirements
