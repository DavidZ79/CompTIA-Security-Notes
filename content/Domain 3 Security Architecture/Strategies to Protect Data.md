## Regulated Data Types

Categories of data that require special protection under various regulations:

<details>
<summary><strong>Personal and Sensitive Data</strong></summary>

*Types of data requiring enhanced protection*

**Personal Information:**
- **PII** - Personally Identifiable Information
- **PHI** - Protected Health Information
- **Biometric Data** - Fingerprint, facial recognition, etc.
- **Consumer Data** - Customer information and preferences

**Business Data:**
- **Financial Data** - Banking and payment information
- **Trade Secrets** - Proprietary business information
- **Intellectual Property (IP)** - Patents, copyrights, trademarks
- **Proprietary Data** - Internal business processes and data

**Legal and Government Data:**
- **Legal Data** - Legal documents and proceedings
- **Government Data** - Classified government information
- **Customer Data** - Client and customer information

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Regulatory Frameworks</strong></summary>

*Key regulations governing data protection*

**International Regulations:**
- **GDPR** - General Data Protection Regulation (EU)
- **Data Protection Act 1998** - UK data protection law

**US Regulations:**
- **HIPAA** - Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act
- **CCPA** - California Consumer Privacy Act
- **SOX** - Sarbanes-Oxley Act
- **GLBA** - Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act

</details>

## Data Classification

Systematic approach to categorizing data based on sensitivity and protection requirements:

<details>
<summary><strong>Government Classifications</strong></summary>

*Official government data classification levels*

**Classification Levels:**
- **Top Secret** - Highest level of classification
- **Secret** - Highly sensitive information
- **Confidential** - Sensitive but not secret information
- **Unclassified** - Public or non-sensitive information

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Non-Government Classifications</strong></summary>

*Corporate and organizational data classification*

**Classification Levels:**
- **Confidential/Proprietary** - Highly sensitive business information
- **Private** - Internal use only information
- **Sensitive** - Information requiring protection
- **Public** - Information that can be freely shared

</details>

## Data States

Different states of data requiring specific protection strategies:

<details>
<summary><strong>Data State Categories</strong></summary>

*Different states of data and their protection needs*

**Data States:**
- **Data at Rest** - Stored data requiring encryption and access controls
- **Data in Transit** - Data being transmitted requiring encryption
- **Data in Use** - Data being processed requiring memory protection
- **Data Sovereignty** - Data subject to specific country regulations
- **Geolocation** - Physical location of data storage

</details>

## Data Protection Methods

Technical and administrative controls for securing sensitive data:

<details>
<summary><strong>Encryption and Hashing</strong></summary>

*Cryptographic methods for data protection*

**Protection Methods:**
- **Encryption** - Converting data to unreadable format
- **Hashing** - Creating fixed-length data fingerprints
- **Tokenization** - Replacing sensitive data with tokens
- **Obfuscation** - Making data difficult to understand

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Access and Location Controls</strong></summary>

*Methods for controlling data access and location*

**Control Methods:**
- **Geographic Restrictions** - Limiting data storage locations
- **Permission Restrictions** - Controlling who can access data
- **Segmentation** - Isolating data in separate systems
- **Masking** - Hiding sensitive data in non-production environments

</details>

> [!note] Vocab Bank
> - **PII** - Personally Identifiable Information, data that can identify an individual
> - **PHI** - Protected Health Information, healthcare data under HIPAA
> - **GDPR** - General Data Protection Regulation, EU privacy law
> - **HIPAA** - Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act, US healthcare privacy law
> - **CCPA** - California Consumer Privacy Act, California state privacy law
> - **SOX** - Sarbanes-Oxley Act, US financial reporting and security law
> - **GLBA** - Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act, US financial privacy law
> - **Tokenization** - Replacing sensitive data with non-sensitive tokens
> - **Data Sovereignty** - Legal requirement that data is subject to laws of the country where it's stored
