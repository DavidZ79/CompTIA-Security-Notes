> [!quote] Definition
> **Threat actors** are individuals or groups that pose a threat to information systems, motivated by various goals such as financial gain, ideology, or disruption. Understanding their types and motivations is crucial for effective cybersecurity defense.

## Threat Actors

Threat actors are categorized based on their origin, resources, and intent. They range from nation-states to insiders within an organization.

<details>
<summary><strong>Threat Actors</strong></summary>

- **Nation-State Actors**: Operate on behalf of a government; highly sophisticated, targeting national interests.
- **Advanced Persistent Threats (APT)**: Well-resourced, skilled groups that conduct prolonged, targeted attacks to cause damage or steal information.
- **Script Kiddies**: Unskilled individuals using pre-made tools, often obtained from the dark web, to launch attacks.
- **Hacktivists**: Attackers motivated by ideological, political, or social causes.
- **Insider Threats**: Individuals within an organization (employees, contractors) who may intentionally or unintentionally cause harm.
- **Organized Crime**: Groups focused on financial gain through cybercrime.
- **Shadow IT**: Unauthorized technology or systems used within an organization, potentially introducing security risks (not always malicious).

</details>

## Threat Actor Classifications

Threat actors can be further classified by their relationship to the organization, available resources, and level of sophistication.

<details>
<summary><strong>Threat Actor Classifications</strong></summary>

- **Internal vs. External**: Originating from within or outside the organization.
- **Resource Level**: Well-resourced (ample funding, tools, expertise) vs. limited resources.
- **Sophistication**: Highly skilled and organized vs. less sophisticated attackers.

</details>

## Motivations

Threat actors are driven by a variety of motivations, from financial gain to ideology or revenge.

<details>
<summary><strong>Motivations</strong></summary>

- **Financial Gain**: Theft, fraud, ransomware, or other profit-driven attacks.
- **Espionage**: Stealing sensitive or confidential information for competitive or strategic advantage.
- **Data Exfiltration**: Unauthorized transfer of data out of an organization.
- **Service Disruption**: Causing downtime or denial of service to disrupt operations.
- **Blackmail/Extortion**: Demanding payment or action in exchange for not releasing or damaging data.
- **Ideological/Political Beliefs**: Driven by ethics, philosophy, or political agendas.
- **Revenge**: Retaliation against an organization or individual.
- **Chaos/Disruption**: Causing disorder or confusion without a clear financial or ideological motive.
- **Warfare**: Cyber operations as part of military conflict.

</details>
