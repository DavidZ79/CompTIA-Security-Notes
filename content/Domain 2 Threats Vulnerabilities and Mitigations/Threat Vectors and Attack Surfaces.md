> [!quote] Definition
> **Threat vectors** are the paths or methods attackers use to gain unauthorized access to systems, while the **attack surface** is the total set of points where an attacker can try to enter or extract data. Understanding both is essential for reducing risk and improving security posture.

## Message-Based Threat Vectors

Message-based vectors use communication channels to deliver malicious content or deceive users.

<details>
<summary><strong>Message-Based Vectors</strong></summary>

- Email
- Short Message Service (SMS)
- Instant Messaging (IM)

</details>

## Image, File, and Voice-Based Vectors

Attackers may use images, files, or voice calls to deliver malware or extract information.

<details>
<summary><strong>Image, File, and Voice-Based Vectors</strong></summary>

- Image-Based
- File-Based
- Voice Call
- Removable Device

</details>

## Vulnerable Software

Exploiting weaknesses in software is a common attack vector.

<details>
<summary><strong>Vulnerable Software & Scanning</strong></summary>

- Vulnerable Software
- Client-based scanning: operates on client device
- Agentless scanning: scan without installations (Nmap, Wireshark)

</details>

## Unsecure Networks

Unsecure or poorly configured networks can expose systems to attack.

<details>
<summary><strong>Unsecure Networks</strong></summary>

- Wireless networks: unsecure. Disable Service Set Identifier (SSID) broadcast, MAC filtering
- Wired networks
- Bluetooth: Personal Area Network (PAN)
- Open Service Ports: Can result in unauthorized access and the execution of malicious code
- Default Credentials

</details>

## Supply Chain Attacks

Attackers may target third-party providers or vendors to compromise an organization.

<details>
<summary><strong>Supply Chain</strong></summary>

- Managed Service Providers (MSPs): third party that fulfills all a company's IT needs
- Vendors
- Suppliers

</details>

## Human Vectors / Social Engineering

Social engineering exploits human behavior to bypass security controls.

<details>
<summary><strong>Human Vectors / Social Engineering</strong></summary>

- Phishing: Untargeted deception to lure victims for their personal information
- Spear Phishing: Targeted phishing for higher-ups
- Smishing: SMS phishing
- Misinformation / Disinformation: manipulate perceptions and decisions
- Impersonation: adopt a false identity
- Business Email Compromise: Use compromised email for financial fraud
- Pretexting: fabricating a scenario to get information
- Watering Hole Attacks: compromise legitimate websites
- Brand Impersonation
- Typosquatting: Registered domains with subtle differences to the real domain

</details>
